    Q3 Product Strategy

    Scaling the core platform while entering the enterprise segment without losing the speed, clarity, and ease of use that made the product win in the first place.

    Strategic Thesis

    Enterprise expansion should feel like a deepening of the product, not a costume change. Every operational investment in Q3 must preserve our strongest signal: low-friction adoption.

    Context

    Strategic Overview

    Our platform has reached a meaningful inflection point: strong mid-market pull, disciplined product focus, and a clear enterprise wedge grounded in onboarding simplicity.

    With 12,000 active teams and accelerating expansion revenue, we now have proof that the product can move up-market without a full repositioning. The challenge is sequencing: deepen the platform, expand go-to-market coverage, and strengthen the technical foundation in a way that compounds instead of fragments.

    This strategy is intentionally narrow. We are not chasing a broad enterprise feature set. We are tightening around the handful of investments that reduce buyer anxiety, unlock larger contracts, and preserve our operational speed.

    Key Insight: Enterprise prospects consistently cite our onboarding clarity as the reason they trust the product quickly. The Q3 roadmap should reinforce that emotional response, not bury it under complexity.

    Guiding Principles

    • Ship in increments; avoid quarter-long product bets with no intermediate proof.
    • Measure adoption within two weeks of launch and kill ambiguity early.
    • Enterprise capabilities should sharpen the core product, not sit beside it.
    • Protect p95 performance so growth does not tax the reading experience of the product.

    Go-to-Market Process

    A high-conviction path from first touch to expansion, designed to feel guided rather than sales-driven.

    1
    Inbound Lead Website, referral, or content creates initial consideration.
    2
    Guided Trial A concise 14-day experience proves speed-to-value quickly.
    3
    Sales Review Demo, security validation, and technical confidence building.
    4
    Contract Procurement lands with minimal implementation anxiety.
    5
    Expansion Usage growth, upsell, and referrals extend the account naturally.
